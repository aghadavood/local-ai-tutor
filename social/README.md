# X (Twitter) Playbook

How each episode becomes a week of content. The repo is your proof; X is your distribution.

## The weekly rhythm (per episode)
- **Drop day:** post the episode **thread** (template in `threads/`). Put the video link in a *reply*, not the first tweet — X suppresses posts with external links, so the main thread must stand alone as value.
- **Mid-week:** one **short clip** of the most surprising failure (e.g., a model inventing fake Persian grammar). Failure clips get shared far more than success clips.
- **Anytime:** re-post the updated **leaderboard image**. This is the recurring asset people follow the series for.
- **Daily:** reply thoughtfully to bigger local-AI and language-learning accounts. Early on, good replies on big accounts beat your own posts for reach.

## Format rules that matter
- First tweet = the hook + the stakes. No link. Make it work as a standalone.
- One concrete idea per tweet. Screenshots > text-only every time.
- Always show the machine specs somewhere — the "weak laptop" identity is your brand.
- Pin your intro tweet (see `threads/pinned-intro.md`).

## Cadence (solo, sustainable)
1 video/week · 1 thread/week · short posts most days · daily replies. Consistency beats volume — don't burn out chasing 5 posts a day.

## Linking the repo
- Put the GitHub repo link in your X bio and in the *reply* under each thread.
- Each thread ends with: "Run the exact same test yourself 👇 [repo link]". Reproducibility is your credibility.
